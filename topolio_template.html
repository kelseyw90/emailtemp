About the Cross Makers

Meet the Founder

Clayton Kent of Seward, Neb., discovered a simple symbol that brings comfort to the hurting, the ill, the dying and the bereaved — the cross.

 

“You can’t take anything with you. But you can hold the cross in your hand and it brings great comfort." 

- Clayton Kent

The Cross Makers of Seward

The Cross Makers of Seward, Nebraska are a group of approximately 40 men and women who meet several times per week and volunteer time and talent to create these hand-crafted wood palm crosses to extend God's richest blessings to you. To date, the Cross Makers have made over 160,000 crosses and have shipped them all over the world.

How the Crosses are Made

Most of the logs, primarily cedar and some oak and walnut, are donated by local folks. Each cross is handmade to fit the hand and no two are alike. There are two sizes, 4 inches (medium) and 2.25 inches (small). The lower limb of the cross is curved slightly to better fit the shape the recipient’s palm.

The Comfort of the Cross

The crosses bring comfort as they are held in the hand helping one to acknowledge the blessings and love of Christ. These crosses have brought comfort to those in the hospital, nursing homes, and in the military. They make a heartfelt gift to those suffering from illness, injury, or those who are anxious or worried. A card accompanies each cross to explain its purpose.
 

Empower Our Mission; Donate Today!

By supporting the Seward Cross Makers, you’re not only acquiring a unique piece of craftsmanship but also contributing to a thriving community initiative that uplifts those in need. Funds made from the sales of the crosses go strictly toward the cost of materials and equipment and to cover expenses like utilities, equipment, materials, and building renovation. Anything extra is passed on to the community. The Cross Makers board of directors has made gifts to local libraries, scholarship funds, food banks, and other worthy causes.
